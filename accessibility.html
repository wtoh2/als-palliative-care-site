Accessibility & Using This Site

This site is designed to support eye-tracking systems and other assistive technologies that act like a mouse or keyboard. The goal is to enable accessibility through native user interface design, while complementing your device’s own accessibility settings.

How to Navigate the Site

  • All clickable elements are presented as large selectable buttons.
  • Focusing on a button highlights it, but activation only occurs when your device sends a click/selection command.
    • A visible highlight / outline change indicates when a button is focused.
    • A yellow ring appears to confirm when a button has been activated.
  • Where needed, vertical scroll buttons ( ▲ ▼ ) are provided as an alternative to continuous scrolling.
  • Take your time. Nothing happens automatically or without your input.

Keyboard Navigation

If you use a keyboard or keyboard-based control:

  • Press Tab or Shift + Tab to move between buttons.
  • Press Enter to activate a button or link.

Feedback & Accessibility Issues

If you have trouble using the site, notice something could work better, or have suggestions for improving accessibility, please contact the site managers.
